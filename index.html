About Me

In May 2020, I graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with bachelor's degrees in music and anthropology. In addition to my major coursework, I took computer science courses in java and web development. I enjoy spending my time traveling outdoors and playing music. While I was UNC, I got the chance to perform with multiple groups from North Carolina all the way to Athens, Georgia.

Portfolio

Yep Roc Records

Redeye Worldwide

As a remote intern for Yep Roc Records and Redeye Worldwide, I assisted the project manager in marketing strategies and search optimization for various records and music videos. Alongside another intern, I optimized videos for Yep Roc Records and Studio One from 2009 to the present. This included content from Bob Marley, Mandolin Orange, Nick Lowe, and Eli Paperboy Reed.

I also created social media advertisements for recent releases on the Yep Roc website, posted on Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram.

On Bandcamp, I optimized digital and physical album descriptions to promote artist profiles. I also helped to organize and publish a record sampler that included tracks from artists such as Aiofe O'Donovan, Chuck Prophet and Blitzen Trapper.

The Daily Tar Heel

University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

As a writer for the Arts & Culture Desk for the Daily Tar Heel (DTH), I directed my focus towards music in the Triangle area. I wrote event previews, Q&As, and full features on artists such as The Motet, Alo Ver, Amjad Ali Khan, and Rachel Despard.

I wrote weekly articles to be published online, conducting both in-person and phone interviews with artists, management, and venue operators. At the end of the week, I met with editors to review and finalize the content before submitting to management.
